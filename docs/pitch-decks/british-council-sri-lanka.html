Slide 2 — The opportunity

  • Sri Lanka has strong and sustained demand for IELTS preparation across study, migration, and career pathways.
  • Learners need volume of realistic practice across all four papers, especially Speaking and Writing, where feedback is slow and expensive to scale.
  • Centres need visibility: who practised, how often, and where cohorts struggle — without adding heavy marking load to teachers.

Speaker note: This slide is about their world, not your product yet.

Slide 3 — The gap

Need Typical reality
Unlimited realistic mocks Limited classroom time; generic PDFs
Immediate feedback on Writing / Speaking Bottlenecked by human capacity
Speaking under exam conditions Hard to schedule 1:1 mock interviews at scale
Admin oversight across branches / intakes Fragmented tools and spreadsheets

eLanguage Center is built to close this gap as a B2B platform licensed to the institution, not a consumer app bolted on ad hoc.

Slide 4 — What eLanguage Center is

  • Web-based IELTS preparation SaaS for organizations (per-seat subscription + usage quotas).
  • All four sections: Reading, Listening, Writing, Speaking — Academic and General Training (learner-selectable).
  • Modes: section practice and full timed mock tests (exam simulation).
  • AI-generated test content with a quality gate (content pool moderation before items go live).
  • AI-driven grading for Writing and Speaking against IELTS criteria; Speaking as an interactive voice conversation with an AI examiner, recordings stored for audit and (on roadmap) human upgrade.

Speaker note: Clearly state this is preparation, not the official test — complements BC’s courses and resources.

Slide 5 — Why British Council Sri Lanka first

  • Mission fit: English, education, and learner outcomes — aligned with how BC positions IELTS support worldwide.
  • Trust: A pilot with BC raises the bar for quality, safeguarding, and academic integrity from day one.
  • Scale insight: You get real classroom and centre usage to harden product, quotas, and support — BC gets early influence on roadmap (e.g. reporting, reviewer workflows).
  • Regional signal: Success in Sri Lanka is a credible reference for other BC teaching centres and partners.

Slide 6 — Product snapshot (learner)

  1. Invite → onboard → choose Academic or General Training.
  2. Dashboard: activity, suggested next step, band trend over time.
  3. Choose section practice or full mock.
  4. Submit → AI grades → criterion-level feedback (Writing / Speaking) + section scores.
  5. History and weak-area hints to guide what to practise next.

Slide 7 — Product snapshot (organization)

  • Org admin: invite learners (single + bulk CSV), seat usage, activity log (who, what section, when, scores).
  • Configurable quotas: per-org seats; per-user daily/monthly AI limits enforced server-side (cost control and fair use).
  • Multi-tenant isolation: data scoped per organization — suitable for “British Council Sri Lanka” as tenant.

Speaker note: Emphasize operational control: BC stays in charge of who gets access.

Slide 8 — Differentiation

Theme eLanguage Center
Speaking Conversational voice examiner (Parts 1–3), not only text prompts
B2B-first Built for licensed seats, bulk onboarding, and logs — not only B2C
Cost discipline Cached content pool, quotas, model tiering for generation vs grading
Roadmap to human assurance Phase 2: reviewer/teacher role, queue, override of AI bands on Speaking recordings

Speaker note: BC cares about reputation — lead with governance and path to human oversight.

Slide 9 — Trust, integrity, and compliance

  • Not a substitute for the official IELTS test — practice and feedback only; marketing and UI must stay unambiguous.
  • Data protection: architecture aims for GDPR-style deletion paths, tenancy isolation, secure recording access (signed URLs, retention policies — default retention configurable, e.g. 90-day discussion).
  • Quality: generated tests pass a moderation step before wide rotation; room to align with BC content standards in a pilot.
  • Accessibility: WCAG 2.1 AA as a design target for serious institutional rollout.

Speaker note: Offer to walk through DPA, retention, and Sri Lanka / UK data flow with their risk team.

Slide 11 — Roadmap relevant to BC

  • MVP v1: All four sections, AI grading, Speaking voice + recordings, org admin, quotas, super-admin operations and content pool moderation.
  • Phase 2 (high interest for BC): Human reviewer workflow, cohort analytics, exports (CSV/PDF), optional SSO, optional per-org branding.
  • Explicitly out of scope: Live human tutoring marketplace — you are not competing with Italki-style models.

Slide 12 — Technology

  • Modern web stack (Next.js, TypeScript), PostgreSQL, multi-tenant data model, PWA-ready front end.
  • AI: routed stack for generation vs grading (cost and quality separation); voice via established realtime APIs; monitoring and error tracking as standard ops.

Speaker note: BC technical teams may ask where data sits — prepare hosting region and subprocessors list.

Appendix A — Executive summary (email follow-up)

Subject: Pilot proposal — eLanguage Center for British Council Sri Lanka IELTS preparation

eLanguage Center is a B2B web platform for IELTS preparation: all four skills, Academic and General Training, section and full mock practice, AI grading, and a conversational AI Speaking examiner with stored recordings. Organizations license seats with quotas and receive admin tools for invites, usage, and activity.

We are seeking British Council Sri Lanka as our first institutional customer in a structured pilot: limited cohort, clear metrics, and joint learning. The pilot strengthens learner practice and visibility for coordinators while helping us harden security, pedagogy, and reporting to institutional standards. We welcome alignment on data protection, academic integrity messaging, and a roadmap toward optional human review of Speaking outputs.

Appendix B — Objection handling

Objection Response direction
“AI grading is not official.” Agree. Position as formative practice; Phase 2 human override where needed.
“We already have materials.” Position as scalable layer — more attempts, faster loop, logs — not a replacement for your syllabus.
“Data residency.” Share architecture and subprocessors; discuss hosting region and retention.
“You’re early-stage.” Honest pilot with capped cohort, exit criteria, and shared risk — pair with responsive support and weekly reviews.
